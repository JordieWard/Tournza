We use a small number of processors only to deliver the service: our form provider (Formspree) transmits form submissions to us, and our payment provider (Stripe) processes membership payments.     6. How long we keep it

    We keep your personal data only for as long as necessary for the purposes above and to meet our legal, accounting and regulatory obligations under applicable UK legislation, after which it is securely deleted. Records tied to payments are retained for the period required by law.

    7. How we protect it

    Data stored locally is held on a password-protected, encrypted device with access limited to authorised people, and backed up securely. We review access regularly.

    Under UK GDPR you can ask us to access, correct, delete, restrict or port your data, and object to certain processing. You can withdraw consent at any time.     Fair play

    Fair Play Rules

    Tournza runs on trust and a clear camera. These rules keep every regional event fair. By entering an event you agree to follow them.

    1. Setup & equipment

    • Regulation board: bullseye 1.73 m from the floor; oche (throw line) 2.37 m from the face of the board.
    • Upload a throw-line photo before your first event so officials can confirm your distance.
    • Use a steel-tip or soft-tip board appropriate to the event listing, in good condition with clear numbers.

    2. Camera

    • Your webcam must show both the board and your throwing action clearly for the whole match.
    • Keep the board well lit and unobstructed. Don't move or re-angle the camera mid-leg.
    • Officials may ask for a quick camera/oche recheck at any time.

    3. Match play

    • 501, double-out. Events are a group round robin into a knockout to the final, capped at 36 players.
    • Call your score after each visit; your opponent confirms. Flag any disagreement before throwing again.
    • Be checked in and ready before check-in closes (10 minutes before start) or your place may go to a reserve.

    4. Conduct

    • Respect opponents, officials and other players. Abusive or threatening behaviour isn't tolerated.
    • Play your own darts — no coaching, prompts or assistance during a leg.

    5. Integrity & anti-cheating

    • No score manipulation, editing the feed, or misrepresenting throws.
    • We run random checks. Suspected breaches are reviewed against the match recording.
    • Confirmed cheating can mean disqualification, forfeiting your buy-in and any prize, and suspension or a ban.

    6. Disputes

    Raise an issue with the official during the match, or email admin@tournza.co.uk afterwards with your event and the time of the incident. We review the recording where available; the official's final decision stands.

    7. Eligibility

    Events are for verified members aged 18 or over with a cleared age and camera check. See our Terms and Privacy Policy.
